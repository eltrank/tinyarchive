(3) House Slipper: ü©∞

There exists a prevalent unspoken no-shoes-in-the-house rule across most Asian cultures: no shoes in the house. Wearing outdoor shoes indoors is almost culturally illegal. The main reason for shoe removal is cleanliness, but it is also a cultural way of showing respect. The ritual of removing one‚Äôs shoes before entering a home is symbolic of leaving the world outside and transitioning to the sanctuary of one‚Äôs home (Montalvo, et al., 2006). Most Asian families continue the tradition of taking off their outside shoes. Some even change into their indoor shoes, typically accompanied by acupressure massage soles that improve blood circulation. Funny enough, these indoor shoes are not only worn as shoes but also utilized as effective discipline tools, often used by loving parents to stimulate a whooping to create an image of authority in the house.
